3.3. KKT条件¶

Karush–Kuhn–Tucker (KKT) 条件给出了一类约束优化问题的解的约束, 通过这些约束可以求解优化问题.

3.3.1. 概念与内涵¶

考虑如下非线性最小或最大化问题

\[{P}_0 : \begin{array}{l}{\min_{\bm x} f({\bm x})} \\ {\text {s.t.}} \left\{\begin{array}{c}{g_{i}({\bm x}) = 0, i=1,2, \ldots, M_g} \\ {h_{j}({\bm x}) \leq 0, j=1,2, \ldots, M_h}\end{array}\right. \end{array} \]

其中, \({\bm x}, {\bm \alpha}, {\bm \beta}\) 为待求变量. \({\bm \alpha}, {\bm \beta}\) 为由KKT乘子组成的向量, 上式转化为拉格朗日函数

(3.2)¶\[{P}_1 : L({\bm x}, {\bm \alpha}, {\bm \beta}) = f({\bm x}) + \sum_{i=1}^{M_g}{\alpha_i}g_i({\bm x}) + \sum_{j=1}^{M_h}{\beta_j}h_j({\bm x}) \]

则其解满足如下KKT条件

(3.3)¶\[\begin{aligned} \nabla_{{\bm x}} L({\bm x}^{*}, {\bm \alpha}^{*}, {\bm \beta}^{*}) &=0 \\ \beta_{j}^{*} h_{j}({\bm x}^{*}) &=0, j=1,2, \ldots, M_h \\ g_{i}({\bm x}^{*}) &=0, i=1,2, \ldots, M_g \\ h_{j}({\bm x}^{*}) & \leq 0, j=1,2, \ldots, M_h \\ \beta_{j}^{*} & \geq 0, j=1,2, \ldots, M_h \end{aligned} \]

其中, \({\bm \alpha}^{*}=[\alpha_1^{*},\alpha_2^{*},\cdots,\alpha_{M_g}^{*}]^T\), \({\bm \beta}^{*}=[\beta_1^{*},\beta_2^{*},\cdots,\beta_{M_g}^{*}]\), \({\bm x}^{*}=[x_1^{*},x_2^{*},\cdots,x_{N}^{*}]\).

KKT条件是使一组解成为最优解的必要条件, 当原问题是凸优化问题的时, KKT条件也是充分条件.

提示

当 \(M_h = 0\) 即不含不等式约束时, 一般称为拉格朗日乘子法.
